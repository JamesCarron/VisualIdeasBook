\newpage
\renewcommand{\theposttitle}{The power of mistakes and discipline}
\renewcommand{\thepostdate}{2026-04-15}
\vspace*{\fill}
\begin{center}
\begin{minipage}{0.92\textwidth}
\small
The gold standard of showing the power of compounding: \\
\textbf{the exponential curve.}

It’s a line that starts almost flat, growing so slowly it feels like nothing’s happening at all (this is where most people quit). But then, the sweet, sweet fruit of all that effort pays off in an “everything, everywhere, all at once” moment that pushes your results to take off!
\end{minipage}
\end{center}
\vspace{4mm}
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth, height=0.24\textheight, keepaspectratio]{C:/GitHub/VisualIdeasBook/data/images/e8afd1bcc58679befd1678ab97648ea7.png}
\end{center}
\vspace{5mm}
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth, height=0.24\textheight, keepaspectratio]{C:/GitHub/VisualIdeasBook/data/images/e2cbc8f2c5ed68ff6bf16da538aa4cd6.png}
\end{center}
\vspace{2mm}
\begin{center}
\begin{minipage}{0.92\textwidth}
\small
So the theory goes: if you get 1\% better everyday for a year, you’ll be 37X better than you were at the start. That’s compounding.

The exponential curve shows how invisible your progress feels at the beginning of anything new. But it also reminds us that small, consistent effort builds on itself over time into a burst of growth that never stops.

By the way if you like stuff like this, Cohort 16 of Thinking in Visual Metaphors starts in late June. \textbf{Sign up for the waitlist here.}
\end{minipage}
\end{center}

\renewcommand{\theposttitle}{The 2 types of talent}
\renewcommand{\thepostdate}{2026-04-29}
\vspace*{\fill}
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth, height=0.55\textheight, keepaspectratio]{C:/GitHub/VisualIdeasBook/data/images/7107960c2e8198a17e4cc17dbd1fa19b.png}
\end{center}
\vspace{2mm}
\begin{center}
\begin{minipage}{0.92\textwidth}
\small
Natural talent and \textbf{Earned talent}.

Of course when you're born with traits that make you "look like a natural", you have a leg up. But if you stop there, you'll never meet your full potential.

When I was a kid, I never thought of myself as someone who could draw. I just enjoyed doing it and thought it was fun.

And you should see the visual metaphors I first posted 4 years ago! Each one took me an embarrassing amount of time and the result wasn't all that great.

But I kept at it, and after 900+ visuals…I got better! That's earned ability and something anyone can do.

It's time to earn your talent.

\textbf{* Side note:} When I posted this visual, there was meaningful dialogue around whether there is such a thing as “natural talent” in the first place. I have my own thoughts, but curious to hear what you think.
\end{minipage}
\end{center}
