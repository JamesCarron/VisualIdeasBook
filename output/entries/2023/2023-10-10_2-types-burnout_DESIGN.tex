Most people misunderstand the term “\textbf{steal like an artist.}” They just “steal” and forget the key part of the phrase: \textbf{“like an artist.”}

\textbf{Artists combine.}

An audience should be able to see influence but not a direct carbon copy. \textbf{ }

As Austin Kleon, the author of \textbf{Steal Like an Artist }notes in his book:

“If you steal from one author, it’s plagiarism; if you steal from many, it’s research.”
