Unfortunately, getting older means also feeling the inevitable loss of the people we love most. And each time feels like the first.

In these moments, I find myself feeling tremendous guilt and shame. Guilt if I try to live a life without them and shame I didn't do more when they were alive.

There's a beautiful TED talk I was reminded of recently off a grief post from Michael J. Boorman (@wisdommadeeasy) that we don't ever really move on from grief. We move forward with it.

A quote I think about that helps me move forward with grief:

"What is grief if not love persevering?"
