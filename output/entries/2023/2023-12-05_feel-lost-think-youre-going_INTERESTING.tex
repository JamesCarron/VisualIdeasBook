\textbf{"Things which matter most must never be at the mercy of things which matter least." }\\
–Johann Wolfgang von Goethe

One of my biggest “aha” moments came from the book, \textbf{“Essentialism” by Greg Mckeown}.

And the insight was simple: \textbf{the word priority is singular. }

Priority used to mean \textit{“the one thing that’s most important.”}

There was no such thing as priorit\textbf{ies} (plural)!

These days, we all want to do everything, everywhere, all at once. But that’s why we feel spread thin…why we’re everywhere and nowhere!

And personally, I know I often spend far too much time focusing on areas I should never have been in the first place!

Which reminds me of another favorite warning when it comes to where to focus your time:

\textbf{“There is nothing quite so useless, as doing with great efficiency, something that should not be done at all.”}
